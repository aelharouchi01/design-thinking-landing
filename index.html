The Program

Solve complex problems, then teach your teams to do the same.

This program equips you to solve complex problems and drive innovation, then to teach that capability back to your teams as a certified trainer. It is built around the three stages of Design Thinking: Immerse, Invent, and Implement. Level 1 builds your foundation; Level 2 applies it to a real organizational challenge, captured in a white paper.

What you will achieve

  • Understand users in depth to surface needs they cannot articulate themselves.
  • Frame sharp, evidence-based problem statements and generate user-centered solutions.
  • Prototype, test, and iterate ideas into real-world solutions.
  • Facilitate teams through every stage of Design Thinking and lead the method with confidence.

What you walk away with

  • The Certified Design Thinking Trainer credential from GIMI.
  • A completed white paper applying Design Thinking to a real challenge (Level 2).

The Curriculum

Three stages, six disciplines, one iterative loop.

Design Thinking moves through three stages, each with its disciplines. The work is iterative: you cycle back as often as the problem demands, testing every promising idea against three questions throughout.

DesirableDo users actually want it?
FeasibleCan it be built?
ViableDoes it work for the business?
01
Stage One

Immerse

Understand the problem

1

Understand

Engage directly with users to build empathy and surface the tacit needs people cannot put into words.

2

Map

Synthesize observations into clear patterns, pain points, and a sharp view of where the opportunities lie.

02
Stage Two

Invent

Define and create

1

Define

Reframe the challenge into a precise, evidence-based problem statement, so the team solves the right problem.

2

Ideate

Generate a wide range of solutions through divergent thinking, then converge on the ideas with the strongest potential.

03
Stage Three

Implement

Bring ideas to life

1

Prototype

Build quick, low-cost mock-ups of your best ideas so they can reach real users fast.

2

User Test

Gather honest feedback from users and stakeholders, and let evidence guide the next iteration.

3

Iterate

Cycle back through Immerse and Invent as testing reveals what the work still needs.

↻ Design Thinking is a loop, not a line — cycle back through the stages as often as the problem demands.
